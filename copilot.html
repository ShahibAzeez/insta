Microsoft Copilot

Microsoft Copilot is an AI-powered companion that works alongside you in various Microsoft products and across the web, helping you to be more creative, productive, and informed.

Uses of Microsoft Copilot

  • Answering questions and providing information from the web
  • Generating text, including summaries, emails, and creative content
  • Helping you brainstorm ideas and create drafts
  • Assisting with coding tasks in development environments
  • Summarizing documents and meetings
  • Generating images from text prompts
  • Integrating with Microsoft 365 applications like Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Outlook to enhance productivity
  • Providing insights and suggestions based on your data

How to Use Microsoft Copilot

  1. Access Copilot through various entry points:
    • **In Windows:** Look for the Copilot icon on your taskbar (depending on your Windows version).
    • **In Microsoft 365 Apps:** Copilot features are integrated directly into applications like Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Outlook. The specific access method varies by application (e.g., a Copilot button or through contextual prompts).
    • **On the Web:** Go to the Microsoft Copilot website.
    • **In Microsoft Edge:** Copilot (formerly Bing Chat) is integrated into the browser. Look for the Copilot icon in the sidebar.
  2. Once Copilot is open, type your prompt or question in the chat interface. Be clear and specific about what you need help with.
  3. Copilot will generate a response based on your input.
  4. Interact with the response by asking follow-up questions, requesting revisions, or exploring different options.
  5. Utilize the specific Copilot features available within different applications (e.g., summarizing a document in Word, generating formulas in Excel, creating slides in PowerPoint).
  6. Experiment with different types of prompts to discover the full range of Copilot's capabilities.

Paid/Free Status

Microsoft Copilot is available in different forms, with varying paid/free status:

  • **Basic Copilot (Web, Windows, Edge):** Generally offered for free with a Microsoft account.
  • **Copilot for Microsoft 365:** A paid add-on for Microsoft 365 business and enterprise subscribers, offering deeper integration with Microsoft applications and more advanced features.
  • Individual Microsoft 365 plans may also include Copilot features. Check your subscription details for availability.
